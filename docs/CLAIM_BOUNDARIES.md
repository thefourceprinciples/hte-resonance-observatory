# Claim Boundaries

HTE Resonance Observatory is an exploratory research instrument. It can create structured harmonic overlays, visualizations, sonifications, and ledgers. It does not replace conventional chemistry, crystallography, spectroscopy, materials science, or experimental validation.

## Evidence ladder

```text
L0 — Raw input
L1 — Parsed formula
L2 — HTE-derived signature
L3 — Matched to known material data
L4 — Statistical correlation observed
L5 — Experimentally replicated or source-backed claim
```

## Allowed statements

- `SiO2` maps to HTE pitch classes 2 and 8 using `atomic_number mod 12`.
- This run generated a weighted HTE chord of `[2, 8, 8]`.
- The current overlay state is an HTE-derived exploratory label.
- This material should be compared against known material data before property claims are made.

## Not allowed without calibration or external evidence

- HTE proves a material has a specific physical property.
- HTE sonification is the material's literal or natural sound.
- HTE mapping proves superconductivity, transmutation, dark matter, or new physics.
- A symbolic harmonic relation is the same thing as measured experimental behavior.

## Recommended public wording

> HTE Resonance Observatory maps material formulas into harmonic coordinate space, renders those mappings through visualization and sonification, and compares them against source-backed material data and experimental ledgers.

## Core discipline

```text
AI proposes.
Ledger records.
Calibration tests.
Evidence upgrades or downgrades.
The archive preserves the boundary.
```
